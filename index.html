Why Groundwork

Change is made of small, repeatable things.

Most environmental progress doesn't come from one big gesture. It comes from thousands of people choosing the slightly harder, slightly better option — again and again, in the same neighborhood, the same week. Groundwork exists to make those choices visible, so the people doing them know they're part of something larger, and the people watching know what to try next.

How it all works.

Everything you need to know before your first pledge.

When you submit a pledge, it adds a leaf to the growth wall on the homepage and increments the community count. It's a public commitment — visible to your neighborhood — but doesn't require check-ins or proof. The accountability is social, not mechanical.
Yes — pledging, joining campaigns, attending events and reading Field Notes are all free. Donations are optional and go directly toward campaign materials, sapling costs, and small grants for neighborhood organizers.
The Events section lists upcoming gatherings by date. Each event shows its neighborhood and location. You can also check the neighborhood map — dots show which areas have the most active campaigns and regular meetups.
Yes. If you have a specific habit in mind that would benefit your block or neighborhood, reach out via the contact page. We'll help you set a goal, write the campaign brief, and get it listed on the site. Most new campaigns launch within two weeks.
Trees planted and events held are counted directly. CO₂e estimates use published lifecycle figures for each campaign type — for example, the carbon savings per plastic bottle avoided, or per tree at 10-year maturity. All methodology is available on the Ledger methodology page.
Your email is used only to send Field Notes if you subscribe, or to confirm your pledge. It is never shared, sold, or used for advertising. You can unsubscribe from the newsletter at any time via any email footer link.
